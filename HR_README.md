# QA Interview Preparation Guide

## Q.1 Tell me about yourself

**Key Points:**
- Name
- Location and family background
- Education summary
- Work experience
- Relevant skills

**Sample Answer:**

My name is Mihir. I live in Ahmedabad with my family and have completed my Diploma and B.E. in Computer Engineering. I bring over 9 years of automation testing experience, currently working as a Senior QA Engineer at Codal Systems Pvt Ltd since July 2018. I'm proficient in Selenium, Java, Cypress, Playwright, and various automation tools and technologies.

---

## Q.2 Why are you looking for a job change?

**Key Points:**
- Express gratitude for previous experience
- Explain what you learned
- Share reasons for change
- Connect to career goals

**Sample Answer:**

I'm grateful for the opportunities my previous organization provided me. I've learned immensely from that experience. I believe career growth requires continuous learning and development. I'm seeking this opportunity to enhance my skills, broaden my technical expertise, and contribute to a platform that values innovation and professional growth.

---

## Q.3 What are your strengths?

- Self-motivated and hardworking
- Adaptable and optimistic
- Quick decision-maker
- Honest with a positive attitude
- Strong ability to transform work environments into collaborative, supportive teams
- Focused on meeting deadlines and maintaining project momentum

---

## Q.4 What are your weaknesses?

**Acknowledge weaknesses honestly while showing growth:**

- I can be impatient when expecting timely results
- I tend to trust people quickly and struggle to decline help requests
- I'm working on prioritizing my task list and re-evaluating deadlines to manage expectations better

---

## Q.5 What are your career goals?

**Short-term Goal:**
Secure a position at a respected company where I can leverage my skills and advance my career trajectory.

**Long-term Goal:**
Achieve a leadership position within the organization, contributing to its success and growth.

---

## Q.6 What are your salary requirements?

**Guidelines:**
- Fresher: Avoid sharing specific numbers; express willingness to align with company standards
- Experienced: May share expected salary range

**Sample Answer:**

Salary is secondary to my career growth and learning opportunities. I seek a package aligned with industry standards and my experience level that meets my economical needs.

---

## Q.7 Why should I hire you?

**Key Points:**
- Relevant skills and experience
- Technical proficiency
- Reliability and punctuality
- Commitment to organizational goals
- Willingness to exceed expectations

**Sample Answer:**

With 9+ years in automation testing and expertise in modern tools, I can deliver quality assurance solutions. I'm punctual, detail-oriented, and committed to supporting your organization's objectives while maintaining high standards.

---

## Q.8 Why do you want to work with our company?

**Key Points:**
- Research the company
- Highlight specific achievements or values
- Connect to your career goals
- Show genuine interest

---

## Q.9 Why are you interested in our company?

**Sample Answer:**

Your company's industry leadership and impressive track record align with my career aspirations. I've researched your products and vision, and I'm impressed by your team's expertise. I believe my skills would integrate seamlessly with your organization's culture and objectives.

---

## Q.11 How would you act if your senior criticizes your work?

**Sample Answer:**

I view criticism as a learning opportunity. I'd acknowledge the feedback, understand the issue, and ensure it doesn't repeat. Perfection isn't attainable, but continuous improvement is.

---

## Q.12 How would you handle resistance to a new idea or policy?

**Sample Answer:**

I'd recognize that change requires explanation and support. I'd provide evidence of the benefits, address concerns, and demonstrate why the new approach adds value to our process.

---

## Q.13 How would you react if a project deadline changes suddenly?

**Sample Answer:**

I'd verify the reasons for the change, communicate transparently with the team, collaborate on a new timeline, and adjust priorities accordingly while maintaining quality standards.
